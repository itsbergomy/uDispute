Notice of Dispute

A Notice of Dispute is a formal legal notice to each credit bureau under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1681i. It lists the accounts you're disputing and starts the bureau's 30-day investigation clock.

Why no specific inaccuracies yet? Consumer attorneys send the notice first, then follow up with detailed inaccuracy letters 15–30 days later. This is strategic — you put the bureau on notice without tipping your hand on the specific legal arguments you'll make.

What happens next? After sending these notices, the bureau must investigate within 30 days. uDispute will unlock Bureau Assault: Inaccuracy Dispute Letters with specific FCRA violations, legal citations, and case law for each account.
