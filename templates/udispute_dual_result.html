Dual-Letter Strategy

Two letters generated for maximum impact. Letter 1 goes to the credit bureau (CRA) to preserve your § 1681s-2(b) private right of action and start the 30-day clock. Letter 2 goes directly to the data furnisher under Regulation V (12 C.F.R. § 1022.43), bypassing the e-OSCAR system entirely.

🏛️

Letter 1 — To Credit Bureau

Sent to the CRA via certified mail. Preserves § 1681s-2(b) rights and triggers the 30-day investigation clock.

🏦

Letter 2 — Direct to Furnisher

Sent directly to the creditor/collector. Bypasses e-OSCAR entirely under 12 C.F.R. § 1022.43 (Regulation V).

Strategy Tips

  • • Send both letters on the same day via certified mail with return receipt requested.
  • • The CRA letter triggers the 30-day FCRA investigation clock and preserves your right to sue the furnisher under § 1681s-2(b).
  • • The furnisher letter bypasses e-OSCAR entirely — the creditor reads your ACTUAL dispute, not a 2-digit code.
  • • Under Regulation V, the furnisher must investigate and report results to ALL bureaus (§ 1022.43(e)).
  • • If the furnisher ignores the direct dispute, you have evidence of willful noncompliance under § 1681n ($100-$1,000 per violation + punitive damages).
