Dual-Letter Strategy

Two letters, one target. Letter 1 goes to the credit bureau to start the 30-day clock. Letter 2 goes directly to the creditor, bypassing e-OSCAR entirely.

🏛️

Letter 1 — To Credit Bureau

Preserves § 1681s-2(b) private right of action. Triggers 30-day investigation under § 1681i.

🏦

Letter 2 — Direct to Furnisher

Bypasses e-OSCAR under 12 C.F.R. § 1022.43 (Regulation V). Furnisher must investigate and report to ALL CRAs.

How to Use the Dual-Letter Strategy

  • • Send both letters on the same day via certified mail with return receipt requested (USPS Form 3811).
  • • The CRA letter triggers the 30-day FCRA investigation clock and preserves your right to sue the furnisher under § 1681s-2(b) if they fail to investigate.
  • • The furnisher letter bypasses e-OSCAR entirely — the creditor reads your ACTUAL dispute with specific Metro 2 field references, not a 2-digit code.
  • • Under Regulation V (§ 1022.43(e)), the furnisher must report investigation results to ALL bureaus — one letter can fix all three reports.
  • • If the furnisher ignores the direct dispute, you have evidence of willful noncompliance: $100-$1,000 per violation + punitive damages under § 1681n.
  • • Keep copies of both letters and certified mail receipts — these are your evidence if you need to escalate to a CFPB complaint or lawsuit.
